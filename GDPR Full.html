About this research project

Project Title: Exploring the Use of Mental Space as an Assessment Tool/Process Alongside Other Instruments using Subjective Units of Distress (SUD)

Principal Investigators: [Dr Lucas Derks, Dr Rod Waddington]

What personal data we collect

By participating in this research, we will collect the following personal data:

Direct identifiers:

  • Name
  • Signature (typed name)
  • Date of participation
  • Participant code

Research data:

  • Therapy modus
  • Subjective Units of Distress (SUD) scores
  • Pre-session and post-session Mental Space assessment responses
  • Other assessment instrument results

Why we collect and use your data

Primary purpose: To analyse whether Mental Space can be used alongside other instruments for validating Subjective Units of Distress (SUD) for assessment purposes.

Secondary purposes:

  • To contribute to academic research and publications in anonymised form
  • To improve mental health assessment tools and methodologies
  • To meet regulatory and professional requirements

Lawful basis for processing:

  • Article 6(1)(a) GDPR: Your explicit consent
  • Article 9(2)(a) GDPR: Your explicit consent for processing special category data (mental health information)

Your consent

What you're consenting to:

  • Participation in the research project described above
  • Processing of your personal data for research purposes
  • Retention of your data for the specified period
  • Use of anonymised data in research publications and presentations

Your right to withdraw: You can withdraw your consent at any time without giving a reason and without any negative consequences. To withdraw consent, contact us using the details above. Please note that withdrawal will not affect the lawfulness of processing carried out before withdrawal.

How we store and protect your data

Security measures:

  • All data stored on encrypted drives and secure servers
  • Access restricted to authorised research personnel only
  • Password-protected systems with regular security updates
  • Physical documents stored in locked, secure facilities

Access controls:

  • Only named research team members have access to identifiable data
  • Access logs maintained for audit purposes

Data minimisation: We only collect and process data that is necessary for the research objectives.

How long we keep your data

Retention period: Up to 5 years from completion of data collection

Justification: This period allows for:

  • Data analysis and validation
  • Preparation of research publications
  • Compliance with research governance requirements
  • Potential follow-up studies (with separate consent)

Secure disposal: After the retention period, all data will be securely deleted from electronic systems and physical documents will be destroyed using confidential waste procedures.

Who we share your data with

We do not routinely share your identifiable personal data with third parties. However, we may be required to share data in the following circumstances:

  • Legal obligations: Where required by law, court order, or regulatory authority
  • Research collaboration: Anonymised data may be shared with academic collaborators
  • Ethics oversight: Ethics committees may require access for audit purposes
  • Safety concerns: Where there are immediate risks to your safety or that of others

International transfers: Some data processing may involve transfers to countries outside the UK/EU. Where this occurs, we ensure appropriate safeguards are in place under GDPR Article 44-49.

Your rights under GDPR

You have the following rights regarding your personal data:

Right of access (Article 15): Request a copy of the personal data we hold about you
Right to rectification (Article 16): Request correction of inaccurate or incomplete data
Right to erasure (Article 17): Request deletion of your data (subject to research requirements)
Right to restrict processing (Article 18): Request limitation on how we use your data
Right to data portability (Article 20): Request transfer of your data in a structured format
Right to object (Article 21): Object to processing based on legitimate interests
Right to withdraw consent: Withdraw consent for future processing at any time
Rights related to automated decision making (Article 22): This research does not involve automated decision making or profiling.

Research-specific rights

Right to withdraw from research: You can withdraw from the research at any time. This is separate from but related to withdrawing consent for data processing.

Right to research results: You may request information about the general findings of the research (in anonymised form).
